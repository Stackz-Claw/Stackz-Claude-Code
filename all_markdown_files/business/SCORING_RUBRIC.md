# OPPORTUNITY SCORING RUBRIC
*Used by: radar | Every opportunity gets scored before entering the pipeline*

---

## TOTAL SCORE: 0–100

Opportunities scoring **≥ 80** enter the validation pipeline.  
Opportunities scoring **60–79** are logged and monitored.  
Opportunities scoring **< 60** are discarded.

---

## SCORING DIMENSIONS

### 1. REVENUE POTENTIAL (25 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 20-25 | Clear path to $10K+/mo ARR within 12 months. Pricing model proven in adjacent market. |
| 13-19 | $3K-$10K/mo ARR realistic. Revenue model is plausible but needs validation. |
| 7-12 | Under $3K/mo likely, or revenue model unclear. |
| 0-6 | No clear revenue model. Would require significant market education. |

**Questions to ask:**
- What's the pricing model? (SaaS, one-time, usage, marketplace fee)
- Who pays and why? Is this a painkiller or a vitamin?
- What's the realistic TAM for us (not the global TAM — the slice we can reach)?

---

### 2. BUILD COMPLEXITY (20 points)
*Lower complexity = higher score. We want fast MVPs.*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 17-20 | MVP buildable in 1-2 weeks with existing stack (Python/JS, APIs, no novel infra). |
| 11-16 | 3-6 week build. Some new integrations needed. Manageable. |
| 5-10 | 6+ week build, or requires expertise we don't have. |
| 0-4 | Novel technical challenge, hardware dependency, or infrastructure we can't build. |

**Questions to ask:**
- What's the MVP scope? (Not the full vision — the thing that validates the core assumption)
- Can Forge build it with current tools?
- Are there API dependencies that could break us (rate limits, pricing changes, shutdowns)?

---

### 3. COMPETITIVE MOAT (20 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 17-20 | Defensible edge: unique data, proprietary workflow, first-mover in a niche, network effects. |
| 11-16 | Moderate edge: better UX, lower price, better distribution, or faster iteration. |
| 5-10 | Commodity space. Competing on execution only. Possible but hard. |
| 0-4 | Already saturated. A well-funded incumbent can clone this in a week. |

**Questions to ask:**
- What's our unfair advantage? (Distribution, speed, cost, unique data, existing audience)
- How long before a competitor with more resources catches up?
- Is there a second-act (network effects, data moat, integration lock-in) that develops over time?

---

### 4. MARKET TIMING (20 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 17-20 | Clear tailwind: regulation change, new technology, macro shift, or trend just breaking. |
| 11-16 | Stable market with consistent demand. Not riding a wave but not fighting one either. |
| 5-10 | Trend may have peaked. Or market is nascent — too early, high education cost. |
| 0-4 | Declining market, or trend was 2 years ago. |

**Questions to ask:**
- Why is now the right time? What changed?
- Is this a growing, stable, or declining problem?
- Are we too early (no willingness to pay yet) or too late (already commoditized)?

---

### 5. OPERATIONAL FIT (15 points)
*How well does this fit our team's current capabilities and capacity?*

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 13-15 | Perfect fit: uses existing skills, tools, and distribution. Low learning curve. |
| 8-12 | Good fit with manageable gaps. We'd need to learn or hire 1-2 things. |
| 3-7 | Moderate mismatch. Significant new capabilities required. |
| 0-2 | We'd be starting from zero. Not our domain at all. |

**Questions to ask:**
- Does this leverage what we already know?
- Can Forge, Canvas, and Megaphone support this without new tool procurement?
- Does this distract from existing ventures or complement them?
