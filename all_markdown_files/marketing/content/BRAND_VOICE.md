# BRAND VOICE
*Tone, vocabulary, and do/don't examples*

---

## VOICE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Direct and Confident
We say what we mean. No fluff, no hedge words.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "This tool saves you 10 hours/week" | "This tool might help you save time" |
| "We built the best API" | "We think we built a pretty good API" |

### 2. Technical but Accessible
We know our stuff. We explain it clearly.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Our API uses RESTful endpoints" | "We use fancy API stuff" |
| "Built on React + Node" | "Made with cool tech" |

### 3. Minimal Filler
Every word earns its place.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Ship faster" | "Ship faster and more efficiently" |
| "No setup required" | "There's absolutely no setup required" |

### 4. Never Arrogant
Confident but humble. We show, don't tell.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Here's what we built" | "We're the best at this" |
| "Try it free" | "The world's best free trial" |

---

## VOCABULARY

### Preferred Terms
- ship, launch, build
- API, integration, endpoint
- MVP, prototype, test
- revenue, growth, customers
- team, founders, builders

### Avoid
- revolutionary, game-changing
- disrupt, unicorn
- guru, ninja, rockstar

---

## TONE BY CHANNEL

### Twitter/X
- Punchy, under 280 characters
- Start with hooks
- End with engagement questions

### LinkedIn
- Professional, insightful
- Longer form acceptable
- Value-first content

### Email
- Conversational
- Scannable (short paragraphs)
- Clear CTAs

---

## FORMATTING RULES

### Twitter
- Use thread for complex topics
- Numbered lists work well
- Emoji: minimal, purposeful

### LinkedIn
- Short paragraphs
- Bullet points for lists
- No excessive emoji

### Email
- Subject line: specific + intriguing
- Body: scannable
- CTA: single, clear

---

## EXAMPLES

### Product Launch
**Do:**
> "We spent 6 months building the API you've been waiting for. Try it free."

**Don't:**
> "We're so excited to announce our revolutionary new product that will change the way you think about APIs forever!"

### Feature Announcement
**Do:**
> "Dark mode is here. Your eyes will thank us later."

**Don't:**
> "We're thrilled to share that after listening to your feedback, we've decided to implement a dark mode feature which we hope you'll enjoy!"

### Customer Win
**Do:**
> "Acme Corp switched from [Competitor] and saved $5K/month. Here's how."

**Don't:**
> "We're so proud to announce that Acme Corp has chosen our solution!"
