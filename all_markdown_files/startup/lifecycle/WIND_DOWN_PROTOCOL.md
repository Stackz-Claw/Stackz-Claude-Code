# WIND-DOWN PROTOCOL
*How to cleanly shut down a failed venture*

---

## TRIGGERS

A venture may be wound down when:
- 6+ months of declining MRR
- No path to profitability
- Competitive displacement inevitable
- Owner decides to exit
- Stackz approves wind-down

---

## WIND-DOWN DECISION

### Assessment Required
- Financial impact of continuing vs. stopping
- Customer impact
- Team impact
- Brand impact

### Approval
- Founder recommends to Stackz
- Stackz makes final decision
- Document decision rationale

---

## COMMUNICATIONS

### Timeline
- T-30 days: Internal team
- T-14 days: Customers
- T-7 days: Public (if applicable)

### Messaging
- Be direct about reasons
- Express gratitude
- Offer alternatives if possible
- Provide timeline

---

## OPERATIONAL WIND-DOWN

### Week 1: Stop
- [ ] Disable new signups
- [ ] Stop marketing spend
- [ ] Cancel pending contracts
- [ ] Notify vendors

### Week 2: Transition
- [ ] Help customers migrate (if applicable)
- [ ] Export customer data
- [ ] Complete pending work
- [ ] Transfer knowledge

### Week 3-4: Close
- [ ] Disable payment processing
- [ ] Archive codebase
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Release team members

---

## FINANCIAL WIND-DOWN

### Settle
- [ ] Final billing run
- [ ] Collect outstanding invoices
- [ ] Pay final expenses
- [ ] Close vendor accounts

### Report
- [ ] Final P&L
- [ ] Total investment
- [ ] Lessons learned
- [ ] Recommendations

---

## ASSETS

### What to Preserve
- Customer data (required by law)
- Code (for potential sale/archival)
- Documentation (lessons learned)
- Analytics (for post-mortem)

### What to Release
- Domains (unless selling)
- Hosting (cancel)
- Licenses (return/cancel)
- Team (reassign)

---

## POST-MORTEM

After wind-down complete:
1. Document what happened
2. What worked
3. What didn't
4. What we'd do differently
5. Share with Stackz
