## TIER 1 PLATFORMS

---

### 1. Product Hunt
**URL:** https://www.producthunt.com  
**Submit:** https://www.producthunt.com/posts/new  
**Audience:** ~5M monthly visitors, tech-savvy early adopters, investors, press  
**Best for:** B2C SaaS, developer tools, AI products, consumer apps

**Submission Requirements:**
- Product name: 50 chars max
- Tagline: 60 chars max — no buzzwords, be specific
- Description: 200 words max
- Logo: 240x240px PNG
- Gallery: up to 8 images (1270x952px recommended)
- First comment: write as maker, personal story, what problem you solve

**Critical Rules:**
- Launch goes live at 12:01am PT — be ready
- Do NOT ask for upvotes directly (gets flagged)
- Engage with every comment within the first 2 hours
- Schedule for Tuesday–Thursday (Mon and Fri are weakest)
- Having a hunter with followers helps significantly
- "Featured" is automatic if you get enough early momentum

**Upvote Benchmarks:**
- Top 5 of the day: 300+ upvotes
- Top 3: 500+ upvotes  
- #1 Product of the Day: 800+ upvotes
- Product of the Week: usually top daily winner

**Tactics:**
- Post in your network asking people to CHECK IT OUT (not "upvote") at exactly 12:01am PT
- First comment should be a genuine founder story — not marketing copy
- Answer every question in comments personally
- Post update comment at T+6h and T+12h with progress/learnings

### 2. Hacker News (Show HN)
**URL:** https://news.ycombinator.com  
**Submit:** https://news.ycombinator.com/submit  
**Audience:** Engineers, technical founders, VCs, technical press  
**Best for:** Developer tools, technical products, anything with technical depth

**Submission Requirements:**
- Title format: `Show HN: [Your Product] – [What it does in plain English]`
- URL: your product URL (not a landing page — the actual product)
- Text: optional but recommended — tell the technical story

**Critical Rules:**
- HN is anti-marketing — write like you're talking to a senior engineer
- Explain the technical decisions you made and why
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Respond to every technical question
- Do NOT post "Please upvote" anywhere — instant credibility death

**Best time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am ET (US working hours)

**What gets traction on HN:**
- Interesting technical approach ("I built X without a database")
- Genuine indie story ("6 months, $0 funding, here's what I learned")
- Opens with what it does, not what it could do
- First paragraph answers "why does this exist?"

**Benchmark:** 10+ points in first hour = will likely make front page

### 3. Indie Hackers
**URL:** https://www.indiehackers.com  
**Submit product:** https://www.indiehackers.com/products/new  
**Audience:** Bootstrapped founders, SaaS builders, revenue-focused community  
**Best for:** Revenue-generating or revenue-seeking SaaS, micro-SaaS, info products

**Submission Requirements:**
- Product name and description
- Revenue (optional but highly recommended — IH respects transparency)
- Founding story
- Tech stack

**Critical Rules:**
- IH community responds to transparency — share real numbers
- Post in the forum to announce your launch separately from the product listing
- "Milestones" posts (first $100, first $1K MRR) get huge engagement
- The community gives genuine feedback — be ready for it

**High-engagement post formats:**
- "I just crossed $X MRR — here's what worked"
- "Launched 6 months ago with no audience — here's what I learned"
- "I've been building in public — month 3 update"

---

### 4. BetaList
**URL:** https://betalist.com  
**Submit:** https://betalist.com/startups/new  
**Audience:** Early adopters, beta testers, people who sign up for everything new  
**Best for:** Pre-launch and early-launch — excellent for building waitlist

**Submission Requirements:**
- Product name, description
- Website URL
- Logo (400x400px)
- Email for notifications
- Launch status (upcoming / launched)

**Critical Rules:**
- Submit 1-2 weeks before launch for best timing
- BetaList features upcoming products — this is for building waitlist
- Review process takes 2-5 days
- Free tier gets listed; paid gets featured (faster, more prominent)

**Expected outcome:** 50-300 waitlist signups depending on product appeal

---

### 5. Uneed
**URL:** https://www.uneed.be  
**Submit:** https://www.uneed.be/submit  
**Audience:** Curated — quality over quantity, 50K+ monthly  
**Best for:** Utility tools, productivity, developer tools, design tools

**Notes:** Uneed is curator-reviewed — not everything gets in. Quality matters.  
**Expected outcome:** 30-150 signups for a good fit product

### 6. Fazier
**URL:** https://fazier.com  
**Submit:** https://fazier.com/submit  
**Audience:** Growing community, less saturated than PH  
**Best for:** Any SaaS, less competitive than Tier 1

**Notes:** Daily featured product gets newsletter blast. Submit for feature consideration.  
**Expected outcome:** 20-100 signups

---

### 7. Microlaunch
**URL:** https://microlaunch.net  
**Submit:** https://microlaunch.net/submit  
**Audience:** Micro-SaaS builders and buyers  
**Best for:** Micro-SaaS specifically — sub-$50/mo tools, niche tools

**Notes:** Very targeted audience. Lower volume but higher conversion for right product.
